About This Build

What's included

All 12 chromatic keys (C, D♭, D, E♭, E, F, F♯, G, A♭, A, B♭, B), each with Major, Natural Minor, Harmonic Minor, and Melodic Minor — 48 scales total. Every key also has its 5 other diatonic modes (Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian, Locrian), for 60 more — 108 scales in all. Fingering for the 7 "easy" keys (C, D, G, A, E plus their modes) follows the standard scaffold taught in most method books. The other 7 keys (B, F♯, F, B♭, E♭, A♭, D♭) have irregular fingering that was read directly from Alfred's Basic Piano Library rather than assumed.

Key decisions

  • "Db Minor" plays C♯ minor's notes. True D♭ minor needs 8 flats including a double-flat (B𝄫) and is never actually written or played that way — every musician uses the enharmonic equivalent, C♯ minor, instead. Selecting D♭ + Minor/Harmonic Minor/Melodic Minor in the dropdown plays C♯ minor's notes (same pitches, sane spelling).
  • A few mode notes are spelled with a practical enharmonic instead of the "textbook correct" one. Some mode/root combinations (e.g. E♭ Locrian) technically need a double-flat or double-sharp to spell perfectly. In those spots the note is respelled to whatever a musician would actually write (for example E♭ Locrian's 5th degree is shown as "A" rather than a theoretical "B𝄫"). Same idea as F♯ major already spelling its 7th degree "F" instead of "E♯".
  • Modes reuse each root's own confirmed major-scale fingering. e.g. B♭ Dorian uses the same hand shape as B♭ Major, not the fingering of whichever different major scale those notes technically belong to. This keeps the physical hand position consistent for a given root across all of its scale types.

What might not be 100% right

Note pitches and scale names are correct throughout — that part was checked carefully. The part that's less certain is exactly which finger number is shown/spoken at a few spots in the irregular keys (B, F♯, F, B♭, E♭, A♭, D♭) and their modes, where the printed fingering in the source book was hard to read at certain notes. Best guesses were made using standard fingering logic and flagged for later double-checking — it would only ever be a wrong finger number, never a wrong note. Full detail on exactly which notes are flagged, and why, is in FINGERING_REVIEW_LOG.md in the project repo.
